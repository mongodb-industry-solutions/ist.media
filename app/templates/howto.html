Instructions and Talk Track

The purpose of this demo is to show how easily a sophisticated, personalized newspaper experience can be built by leveraging the best features of MongoDB and generative AI (GenAI) to deliver multimodal content across multichannel platforms.

How to use the demo

There are several paths you can follow to conduct your demo. Combine them as you see fit for your meeting with the customer, aligning to their requirements:

  • Document Model: In the Single Post Page, you can click on the article's title to open a browser window which displays the MongoDB Document in a nice, colored fashion. Use this to discuss how to build 360 degree object structures, in this case a document which is capable of storing all of the article's main content fields (id, title, body, date, imageURL), calculated meta data (e.g. read count), generated fields with the help of GenAI (e.g. keywords, sentiment) as well as embeddings for semantic/vector search.
  • Hybrid Search: Use the form at the top of the Home or Single Post page to enter what you are interested about. It can be keyword-based ("Apple AI"), or a full phrase ("what are the recent notable events around Lucid Group"). In the search results, you find some meta information below each item. It is the individual scores of the Full Text and Vector searches, as well as the Time Decay factor (the older the article, the lower the number). Explain to your customer how easily developers can implement such a sophisticated parallel search. You can use this picture for more technical audiences.
  • Multimodal Video Search: On the left main navigation, click Video Search (accessible from the Home page). You can search for something displayed in the video ("basketball"), including written text ("Ted Lasso", "the feeling of joy"), and for words/sentences spoken on the voice track ("car play ultra"). One of two videos will be selected, and they start playing with an appropriate offset to immediately see the sequence in question. To make your life easier, there's a list of preconfigured search terms at the bottom of the page for you to click. This capability of the demo is powered by multimodal embeddings of Voyage AI.
  • Content Suggestions / Related Posts: Click on a news article on the Home page. Show the right column of the page, where three Related Posts are displayed, together with their matching score (between 0 and 1). Explain that this feature involves only MongoDB Vector Search, executed on-the-fly at display time. No LLM required. Further elaborate on the fact that usually these Related Posts are hardcoded by the editor of the article at writing time. Now, with this approach, content is selected dynamically: When looking at older articles, they can automatically be linked to newer related content.
  • Personalization of the Home Page: Click on one or two articles of similar topic (ideally combining this with Hybrid Search, then the job is easier) and then click back to the Home page. The history of the previously read articles is used to show related articles on the Home page, rather than just sorting by newest. Explain that this feature involves a MongoDB Vector Search that is conducted on-the-fly under the hood every time the Home page is accessed. Argue that this is only possible with a good performing search functionality like the one that MongoDB provides.
  • Meta Data Calculation (Sections / Keywords / Sentiment / Popularity): Click on an article. At the top of the right column there is a list of 5-6 keywords displayed. Explain that they have been calculated with the help of an LLM, and then added to the JSON Document, leveraging the flexibility of the MongoDB Document Model. You can click on a keyword to fetch background information about the item (again leveraging the power of GenAI).
    In a similar way, sentiment of the article (positive, negative, neutral) is calculated, and displayed in smiley format. Furthermore, up to three sections are auto-generated for each article, and stored in MongoDB as a JSON list. Another opportunity for you to emphasize the power of the document model, allowing for list structures.
    Finally, popularity of the article is tracked by counting the page impressions, as well as the qualified reads (user scrolls to the end of the article, and stays on the page for longer than 10 seconds). This data will be used for dynamic pricing in a later version of the demo.
  • Content Manipulations (Summary / Translation): Click on an article. At the top of the article's text, you find links to create a summary and to translate to various languages, available from the dropdown list. Explain that these manipulations are done on-the-fly with the help of an LLM.
  • Dossiers / Multimodal & Multichannel Content: On the left main navigation, click to Daily Summary. You will see a summarization of all the recent news, which is generated with the help of an LLM. Close to midnight each working day, the summary is sent by E-Mail as well. Let me know if you want to receive these emails and I can add you to the list. Furthermore, creation of multimodal content is demonstrated: At the top of the content area, you can start listening to a podcast version of the summary. NotebookLM from Google is used for this feature. The podcasts for every workday are uploaded to Apple, so you can also point your customer to that channel for listening in.
  • Search Generative Experiences (SGE): Click to the Insights page in the main navigtion on the left. Use the "Ask Question" form at the top of the page to formulate a question about any news-related topic. Using a RAG Architecture an informed answer to the question is formulated, which takes into account three sources:
    • Knowledge baked into the LLM (available up to its cut-off date)
    • Information from relevant news articles within ist.media (retrieved by MongoDB Vector Search)
    • Information from public web sources, looked up in real-time (in the spirit of Agentic AI)

 

Behind the Scenes

News articles are ingested from RSS feeds every 60 minutes during business hours, content is cleansed (removal of HTML Tags, Ads, Javascript, CSS, ...) and enriched (creation of Main Image) with the help of OpenAI GPT-4o-mini and Dall-E 3, and embeddings are calculated for Semantic Search and RAG.

A number of Content Services are then using the article data stored in MongoDB, leveraging GPT-4o-mini, Google NotebookLM, and soon also Grok 3.

For delivery, content is processed such that it renders nicely on multiple output channels – Desktop Browser, Mobile Phone, E-Mail. In addition, content can be consumed in several formats – text, audio/podcast.

The power of the Document Model can be leveraged for a publishing news website, by jointly storing various attributes of the article in one single place: Main Data, Meta Data, UGC, Logs (what you process togther you should store together).

Hybrid Search can be coded with LangChain in just a few lines of Python code, significantly boosting developer productivity. We are a partner of LangChain, and MongoDB is a first-level citizen in the LangChain library.

To demonstrate the creation of multimodal assets, there's an Apple podcast produced every workday, with news items from MongoDB. NotebookLM from Google is handling the text2audio conversion.

Using a RAG Architecture, the LLM gets latest context about the questions being asked, so it can provide better informed answers, with much less hallucinations.

Why MongoDB

  • Flexible JSON Document Model to store all facets of a news item in one place. This allows for great performance, and it is the most intuitive way to develop the architecture in the first place
  • Ultra-fast, Atlas-integrated Fulltext and Vector Search. With the integration of Voyage AI soon even easier with auto-embeddings
  • AI-model agnostic — free choice of embedding and LL models. Super important in the fast-changing world of AI
  • Deep integration of LangChain for boost in developer efficiency (deliver in time / in budget)
  • Great performance, to track user actions in realtime, and to deliver pages quickly (already a 100ms increased latency severely increments the churn rate)
  • High resilience (99.995%) to provide readers 24x7 with fresh news, all around the world, all days of the week
