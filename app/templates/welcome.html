Welcome!

This application showcases how MongoDB Atlas Vector Search in combination with OpenAI and LangChain can be used to drive personalized experiences for the Media industry.

Please read further to learn what you can do here. In case of questions, or if you have a feature request, or encountered a bug, let me know by mail.

BTW, this page is only displayed once per browser session. You can nevertheless load it again at any time by clicking into the Backstage Area and following the link at the top.

Personalization

When you start using the application in a fresh browser session, and you navigate to the Home Page, nothing is known about you yet (no browsing history, no explicit user profile), therefore eight articles are shuffled out of a few thousand available ones.

You can click on an article to load its content, displayed on the Single Post Page. This will save the article in your browsing history, which is basically a list of previously clicked articles. With every click on an article, the list grows, and information about your interests grows with it. You can check which articles are in your browsing history by navigating to the Backstage Area. From there, it is also possible to delete the history, to start from scratch again.

Every time you navigate to the Home Page while there are articles in your browsing history, the displayed news aren't shuffled, but rather a MongoDB Atlas Vector Search is executed, finding content on-the-fly that is similar (KNN Search, K-Nearest Neighbour) to what is in your history.

Search Form

On the Home Page and on the Single Post Page, you find a search form at the top:

Whatever you send through this form will trigger Vector Search to find semantically close news articles. Don't just use keywords for your search, but also experiment with sending multiple words, or even complete sentences!

Content Suggestions

Another location in the application where MongoDB Atlas Vector Search will be executed is the Single Post Page. It contains a right column with an area called "Related Posts". These are calculated on-the-fly to be similar to the article on screen.
 

 
Once you click on one of these "next best reads", and the article is therefore put into your browsing history, it gets filtered from the "Related Posts" view, to avoid circular referrals, and from the personalized view on the Home Page. It does not hold for the search form, though — an article from the user's history is allowed to appear in search results at any time.

Keyword Generation

The right column of the Single Post Page contains another area called "Keywords". It contains up to six entries that are calculated on-the-fly by calling GPT-3.5 with the following prompt: 

			Return a machine-readable Python list.
			Given the context of the media article,
			please provide me with 6 short keywords
			that capture the essence of the content
			and help finding the article while
			searching the web. Consider terms that
			are central to the article's subject
			and are likely to be imported for
			summarization. Please prioritize names
			of companies, names of persons, names
			of products, events, technical terms,
			business terms over general words.
			Return a machine-readable Python list.

Each keyworld is clickable. This will load the Insights Page, where GPT-3.5 is instructed to provide some background information, using the following prompt: 

			Tell me about the following, writing three paragraphs.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)

When you call the Insights Page without any arguments (e.g. by calling it from the left main menu), you can start experimenting with RAG by entering your question in the search form at the top of the page.

This will instruct GPT-4 to generate an answer in natural language, using a context that is calculated on the fly by calling MongoDB Atlas Vector Search, to find the most relevant articles for the answer:

 
All calculated answers are cached in MongoDB, and are available for easy and fast retrieval in the right column of the page. The Backstage Area has functionality to delete individual cached entries ("History of AI-Generated Insights").
